# Risk Model

High-risk domains:

- health/medicine
- law
- finance/investment
- crime/true crime
- real people
- private facts
- vulnerable groups
- politics/social conflict
- sensitive history
- technical/safety instructions
- copyright and plagiarism

Assess severity by:

- likely reader action
- affected people
- reversibility of harm
- evidence strength
- source currentness
- jurisdiction sensitivity
- privacy/reputation impact
- author expertise
- whether a qualified expert has reviewed the claim

## Risk Levels

| level | meaning | action |
|---|---|---|
| low | minimal harm if wrong | normal sourcing and clarity |
| medium | reader confusion or minor practical harm possible | cite, add limits, fact-check |
| high | reader may make consequential decisions or real people may be harmed | expert/legal review before publication |
| critical | likely medical/legal/financial/safety/reputational harm | stop drafting claim until evidence and review are complete |

## High-Risk Red Flags

- "always", "never", "guaranteed", "cure", "legal loophole", "risk-free", "proven secret"
- claims about living people that imply crime, fraud, abuse, negligence, addiction, diagnosis, or sexual conduct
- advice that bypasses professionals
- outdated law, health, finance, or technical source
- anonymous source as sole basis for serious allegation
- platform rumor treated as fact
