# Expert Review Checklist

Require expert review when:

- readers may change medical, legal, financial, safety, or technical behavior
- named people or organizations are accused or exposed
- technical errors could cause data loss, security exposure, or physical harm
- the writer lacks direct domain expertise
- the audience includes children, patients, victims, survivors, or vulnerable groups
- the manuscript covers contested history or current political/social conflict

## Reviewer Types

| domain | reviewer |
|---|---|
| health | physician, relevant clinician, public-health specialist, pharmacist, or mental-health professional |
| law | licensed attorney in the relevant jurisdiction |
| finance | certified financial professional, accountant, compliance specialist, or domain-specific analyst |
| true crime | media lawyer plus sensitivity/ethics reader where appropriate |
| biography/memoir | media lawyer, fact-checker, sensitivity/privacy reviewer |
| history | historian or subject-matter scholar |
| technical | senior engineer, security reviewer, standards/domain expert |

## Review Package

Output:

- reviewer type needed
- exact claims to review
- source package needed
- source dates and jurisdictions
- manuscript sections affected
- likely reader action
- known uncertainty
- revision required before review

Do not ask an expert to review vague chapters. Send claim-level questions with sources attached.
