# Source Quality Rubric

## Tiers

- A: primary records, laws, regulations, court records, original data, archival documents, official filings, standards.
- B: peer-reviewed research, systematic reviews, expert consensus, official guidance.
- C: expert interviews, professional manuals, institutional reports, publisher or industry documentation.
- D: reputable journalism, trade publishing, edited craft/professional sources.
- E: memoir, personal essay, lived experience, witness account.
- F: social posts, forums, marketing pages, unsourced blogs, platform chatter.

## Evaluation Fields

- source identity
- author expertise
- institution/publisher
- date/currentness
- evidence type
- methodology
- conflicts of interest
- jurisdiction/market
- scope and limits
- counterevidence
- citation usability
- potential reader harm

## Use Rules

- Health/medical claims require current official/professional or peer-reviewed sources and expert review when behavior may change.
- Legal/financial advice requires jurisdiction-specific current official/professional sources and expert review.
- Allegations about real people require primary evidence, corroboration, and legal review.
- Historical claims need primary or reputable secondary sources, with contested interpretations flagged.
- Technical instructions need official docs/specs and tested examples when failure could cause harm.
- Personal experience can use E-tier if framed as personal, not universal.
- F-tier sources are leads, audience-language evidence, or market-signal material, not proof.

## Source Decision

| result | action |
|---|---|
| threshold met | use with citation/notes |
| source useful but weak | use as lead; find stronger source |
| source currentness unclear | verify date before using |
| conflict of interest high | corroborate or exclude |
| potential harm high | require expert review |
| evidence missing | soften, defer, or remove claim |
