# Research Protocols

## Select Methods by Evidence Burden

- Fiction: plausibility research, procedural checks, setting detail, language/register checks, sensitivity/context review.
- Narrative nonfiction: primary documents, timeline, interviews, archive, corroboration, legal/ethics review for real people.
- Practical nonfiction: reader interviews, case studies, method testing, competitor analysis, outcome limits.
- Scholarly/technical: literature review, definitions, standards/specs, expert validation, reproducible examples, citations.
- Visual/hybrid: visual references, rights notes, caption facts, page sequence, accessibility metadata where relevant.
- High-risk professional information: official/current sources, expert review, reader-harm assessment, claim-by-claim source map.
- Korean publication projects: ISBN/metadata checklist, bookstore/platform category scan, Korean style policy.

## Research Plan Fields

- core questions
- claim types
- evidence burden
- methods
- sources to find
- interviews/experts
- field/archive/data work
- competitor books
- bookstore/platform categories
- fact-check targets
- risk register
- output artifacts

## Domain-Specific Minimums

| domain | minimum source plan |
|---|---|
| health/medicine | official or professional source, evidence synthesis where possible, study limits, expert review if advice affects behavior |
| law | jurisdiction, statute/regulation/case source, current-status check, professional review for action guidance |
| finance | regulator/source filings, date, assumptions, risks, fees/taxes/liquidity, no guaranteed outcome language |
| history | primary source or archive plus reputable secondary interpretation and chronology |
| biography/memoir | interview/document map, consent/privacy review, corroboration for serious claims |
| true crime | public records/court documents where available, victim-sensitivity review, legal review for allegations |
| scholarly | literature map, peer-reviewed sources, counterarguments, citation plan |
| technical | official docs/specs, version matrix, tested examples, security/safety notes |

## Korean Market Research

For Korean-language publication or Korean market targeting, include:

- KDC/DDC or institutional subject position when useful
- ISBN/Korean book number and add-on-code planning when near publication
- ONIX-style metadata packet
- current bookstore category paths
- web novel/platform tags if serial fiction
- Korean spelling, spacing, punctuation, loanword, honorific, and dialect policy

## Research Artifacts

Produce at least one:

- source matrix
- interview plan
- archive log
- evidence map
- fact-check sheet
- metadata/category matrix
- risk register
