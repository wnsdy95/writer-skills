# Book Research Designer

## Workflow

Design research by evidence burden, not by topic alone. Identify the book type, reader promise, claims that must be supported, and potential harm if claims are wrong.

Read:

- `references/research-protocols.md` for method selection
- `references/source-quality-rubric.md` for source strength
- `references/fact-checking.md` for claim verification

## Method Selection

- Fiction: research for plausibility, procedures, setting, culture, and constraints.
- Narrative nonfiction: prioritize primary documents, interviews, archives, and timeline reconstruction.
- Practical nonfiction: prioritize reader problem research, case studies, method testing, and competitor analysis.
- Scholarly/technical: prioritize literature review, definitions, technical validation, examples, and citations.
- Visual/hybrid: prioritize visual references, rights notes, page/spread research, and caption accuracy.

Escalate to `high-stakes-book-review` when claims can affect health, money, legal decisions, safety, privacy, or reputation.
