# Continuity Checks

Check for:

- unsupported payoff
- orphan setup
- unfair mystery clue
- red herring with false visible facts rather than false interpretation
- timeline contradiction
- impossible travel/time gap
- character knowledge contradiction
- POV character narrating knowledge they do not have
- relationship state mismatch
- voice drift
- term spelling drift
- Korean spacing/punctuation policy drift
- world-rule contradiction
- rule exception introduced only to solve climax
- retcon without revision queue
- real-world research fact mixed into fiction canon without `source_basis`

Severity:

- blocker: drafting cannot continue coherently
- major: affects plot, reveal, arc, canon, or reader trust
- minor: local detail issue
- style: voice, spelling, register, or convention drift

## Fairness Tests

A clue is fair when:

- the visible detail appears before the reveal
- a careful reader can locate it on reread
- the false interpretation is plausible
- the true meaning does not require hidden off-page evidence
- the solution is not created by accident or sudden intuition

## Knowledge Tests

For each important scene, compare:

- author knowledge
- reader knowledge
- POV character knowledge
- protagonist knowledge
- antagonist knowledge
- other major character knowledge

Flag a contradiction when a character acts on information they have not learned, forgets information without cause, or conceals information from their own close POV narration unfairly.
