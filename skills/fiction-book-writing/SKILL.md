# Fiction Book Writing

## Workflow

Design fiction as a causal system, not a sequence of cool events. Plot and character arc must pressure each other.

Read:

- `references/fiction-craft-system.md` for the overall craft model
- `references/plot-character-arc.md` when designing protagonist, antagonist, or long arc
- `references/scene-design.md` when producing scene lists or chapters
- `references/setup-payoff.md` when designing clues, foreshadowing, reveals, reversals, or endings

Use `fiction-story-bible-manager` when the story is long, serialized, mystery-heavy, timeline-heavy, worldbuilding-heavy, or contains many recurring terms and entities.

## Quality Rules

- Give every major scene a goal, conflict, turning point, information change, and emotional or relationship shift.
- Make every major payoff fair by planting setup first.
- Track what the reader knows separately from what the POV character knows.
- Give world rules costs, limits, and exceptions.
- Avoid deus ex machina, rule drift, filler scenes, and character choices that exist only to serve outline convenience.
