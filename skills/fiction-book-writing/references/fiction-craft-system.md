# Fiction Craft System

Use plot, character, scene, and reader knowledge as one system.

## Source Principles

- Plot must emerge from character action under pressure, not from author convenience.
- A scene should change story state, character state, relationship state, knowledge state, or reader expectation.
- Foreshadowing is a planted future-facing signal; decorative detail becomes setup only when later meaning depends on it.
- A story bible is required for long, complex, mystery-heavy, series, historical, or multi-POV fiction because chat history is not a stable canon store.
- Fair-play mystery requires that solution-dependent clues be available to the reader before the reveal, even if their meaning is misdirected.

## Minimum Design

- premise
- protagonist desire and need
- antagonist/opposition
- world pressure
- central dramatic question
- reader promise
- theme
- major turns
- character arc stages
- scene list
- setup/payoff plan
- knowledge/POV plan
- story bible update request

## Premise Contract

Do not draft before the premise answers:

- Why this protagonist?
- Why now?
- What does the protagonist want?
- What do they need but resist?
- What pressure forces action?
- What will happen if they fail?
- What reader promise must the ending satisfy?

## Character and Plot Unity

For every major beat:

- identify the character desire or fear driving action
- identify the external consequence
- identify the internal pressure
- identify the cost
- identify the next necessary beat

If a beat could happen to any protagonist, redesign it around this protagonist's wound, vow, secret, relationship, or choice pattern.

## Major Scene Requirements

Major scenes must have:

- POV
- location/time
- goal
- conflict
- turn
- new information
- knowledge change
- emotional shift
- relationship shift
- setup or payoff
- canon impact

Reject or redesign scenes that only explain information.

## Genre-Specific Additions

| genre pressure | required control |
|---|---|
| mystery/thriller | fair-play clue matrix, red herring log, reveal dependency check |
| fantasy/SF | world-rule cost, exception, first demonstration, contradiction ban |
| romance | desire/need for each lead, relationship turning points, consent and power dynamics |
| historical fiction | fact/invention boundary, chronology, language/register policy |
| web serial | episode hook, readability, recurring promise, but still causal whole-story arc |
| literary fiction | interior pressure, image/theme pattern, voice discipline, implied causality |

## Revision Order

1. reader promise and ending
2. plot causality
3. character arcs
4. timeline and continuity
5. setup/payoff and clue fairness
6. scene function
7. POV and information flow
8. pacing
9. voice and dialogue
10. Korean style/spelling/punctuation pass when writing Korean
11. line polish
