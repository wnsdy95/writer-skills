# Source Bibliography and Usage Notes

## Purpose

This registry records the external sources used to ground `writer-collections`. The skills should not quote these sources as authority for every project. Instead, they should use them to select the right evidence burden, craft checks, and publishing workflow.

## Source Tiers

| tier | source type | use |
|---|---|---|
| Official/standards | government, national libraries, courts, regulators, standards bodies | metadata, legal/current rules, official definitions, compliance-sensitive facts |
| Scholarly | peer-reviewed journals, academic databases, university guides | research claims, historical/contextual interpretation, domain background |
| Professional/institutional | professional bodies, publishers, editorial institutions, major research organizations | workflow, industry practice, expert consensus |
| Craft/trade | writing schools, editors, author education, genre practitioners | writing heuristics and practical craft diagnostics |
| Market/platform | bookstores, platforms, trend reports, public category pages | discoverability, category language, reader expectations |
| Informal/community | forums, social posts, unsourced blogs | audience vocabulary and leads only, not proof |

## Fiction Craft and Story Bible

| source | use | caution |
|---|---|---|
| [Purdue OWL Fiction Writing Basics](https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/subject_specific_writing/creative_writing/writers/fiction_writing_basics/index.html) | Baseline craft terms: character, plot, conflict, point of view, story progression. | Introductory source; turn into project-specific diagnostic checks. |
| [Purdue OWL fiction pitfalls](https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/resources/writing_tutors/tutoring_creative_writing_students/pitfalls_for_fiction_writers.html) | Revision checklist for showing/telling, scope, development, and plot progression issues. | Designed for tutoring context, not a full novel system. |
| [Oregon State Guide: Foreshadowing](https://liberalarts.oregonstate.edu/wlf/what-foreshadowing) | Definition of foreshadowing as planted future-facing narrative signal. | Use for concept grounding; ledger design is our operational extension. |
| [The Center for Fiction: Plot and Pacing](https://centerforfiction.org/writing-tools/plot-and-pacing/) | Practical lens for plot span, pacing, and avoiding unnecessary backstory. | Trade/craft source; compare with genre demands. |
| [Jane Friedman: The Story Bible](https://janefriedman.com/the-story-bible/) | Story bible as continuity memory for setting, characters, scenes, conflicts, phrases, and details. | Author-business/craft source; adapt for novels and LLM memory systems. |
| [Arc Studio: Season Outlines and Bibles](https://help.arcstudiopro.com/guides/season-outlines-bibles) | Central repository model for arcs, storylines, recurring characters, locations, and key beats. | Screenwriting tool context; translate to prose fiction. |
| [Ronald Knox fair-play detective rules via Gotham Writers](https://www.writingclasses.com/toolbox/tips-masters/ronald-knox-10-commandments-of-detective-fiction) | Historical fair-play principle: central clues should be inspectable by the reader, and solutions should not depend on accident. | Period-specific and partly outdated; use only the fairness logic, not every rule literally. |

## Korean Publishing, Metadata, and Style

| source | use | caution |
|---|---|---|
| [National Library of Korea ISBN/ISSN/UCI system](https://www.nl.go.kr/seoji/) | Official Korean ISBN, ISSN, UCI, and deposit workflow entry point. | Workflow can change; verify before production use. |
| [National Library of Korea ISBN introduction](https://www.nl.go.kr/seoji/contents/S20101050000.do) | Korean book number structure: ISBN plus add-on code, country/group/publisher/title/check elements, reader target code. | Do not infer bookstore category only from ISBN content code. |
| [Korean ISBN and ISSN Manual](https://www.isbn-international.org/sites/default/files/Korean_ISBN_and_ISSN_manual.pdf) | Detailed add-on code structure: reader target, format, and content classification. | Older manual; verify against current NLK pages before final publication. |
| [Publication Distribution Integrated Network metadata service](https://bnk.kpipa.or.kr/home/v3/center/centerGuideServiceMetaData) | Metadata as structured book information for bookstores, libraries, educators, and readers; ONIX-based distribution workflow. | Operational platform source; field requirements may change. |
| [ONIX service guide](https://bnk.kpipa.or.kr/home/v3/center/centerGuideServiceOnix) | ONIX as publishing product information exchange standard across print and digital materials. | Use for metadata planning, not legal compliance advice. |
| [KCI: Korean online bookstore classification study](https://www.kci.go.kr/kciportal/ci/sereArticleSearch/ciSereArtiView.kci?sereArticleSearchBean.artiId=ART001747099) | Evidence that online bookstore categories can align at top level but differ in assignment by book; ISBN content code is not enough for sales categorization. | 2013 study; use as structural caution, not current market map. |
| [National Institute of Korean Language norms](https://korean.go.kr/kornorms/regltn/regltnView.do) | Korean spelling, spacing, punctuation, and language-norm reference. | Dialogue, dialect, voice, and intentional style may override standard prose norms when documented. |
| [KPIPA publication industry survey](https://www.kpipa.or.kr/p/g3_1/140) | Korean publishing market context and industry statistics. | Check license and year before reproducing figures. |
| [KCI: web novel aesthetics and genre hybridity](https://www.kci.go.kr/kciportal/ci/sereArticleSearch/ciSereArtiView.kci?sereArticleSearchBean.artiId=ART002537240) | Web novels as flexible, hybrid, platform-driven genre formations such as romance-fantasy and modern-fantasy. | Academic interpretation; supplement with current platform category scans. |
| [KCI: Korean web novel narrative form](https://www.kci.go.kr/kciportal/ci/sereArticleSearch/ciSereArtiView.kci?sereArticleSearchBean.artiId=ART002199659) | Web novel serialization, readability, mobile format, and episode-level tension as form pressures. | Descriptive and critical; do not reduce all web fiction to one pattern. |

## Health, Law, Finance, and Other High-Risk Domains

| source | use | caution |
|---|---|---|
| [MedlinePlus: Evaluating Health Information](https://medlineplus.gov/evaluatinghealthinformation.html) | Health-source evaluation questions: who runs it, purpose/funding, expert review, sources, currency, and professional-care limits. | Never use writing guidance as medical advice. |
| [Cochrane Evidence](https://www.cochrane.org/evidence) | Systematic reviews and evidence-based healthcare synthesis. | Confirm the review date, population, and whether later guidance supersedes it. |
| [NICE Evidence Standards Framework](https://www.nice.org.uk/what-nice-does/digital-health/evidence-standards-framework-esf-for-digital-health-technologies) | Evidence expectations for digital health technologies and health-intervention claims. | UK framework; adapt to jurisdiction and product type. |
| [Korean Law Information Center](https://www.law.go.kr/) | Korean statutes, regulations, administrative rules, and legal source lookup. | Legal interpretation requires jurisdiction/date and expert review. |
| [Korean Supreme Court legal information portal](https://portal.scourt.go.kr/pgp/index.on?c=900&l=N&m=PGP1001M01) | Court decisions and legal information. | Cases must be checked for current authority and procedural posture. |
| [SEC EDGAR investor guide](https://www.investor.gov/introduction-investing/getting-started/researching-investments/using-edgar-research-investments) | Public company filings and investment research source baseline. | US-specific; pair with Korean FSS/FSC for Korean finance. |
| [Korean Financial Supervisory Service](https://www.fss.or.kr/) | Korean financial supervision, consumer warnings, disclosure context. | Verify current notices and product-specific rules. |
| [Korean Financial Services Commission](https://www.fsc.go.kr/) | Korean financial policy and regulation. | Policy pages are not individualized financial advice. |

## History, Archives, Scholarship, and Technical Standards

| source | use | caution |
|---|---|---|
| [National Archives research portal](https://www.archives.gov/research) | US archival records and research tools. | Primary records require context and provenance checks. |
| [Library of Congress research guides](https://guides.loc.gov/) | Curated research starting points for historical and domain topics. | Guides are paths into sources, not final proof by themselves. |
| [Korean History Database](https://db.history.go.kr/) | Korean historical sources and databases. | Translate and interpret with period expertise when stakes are high. |
| [Korean National Archives portal](https://www.archives.go.kr/) | Korean archival search and topic records. | Record metadata and access context must be preserved. |
| [ACM Digital Library](https://www.acm.org/publications/digital-library) | Computing research literature and bibliographic records. | Do not treat abstracts as full evidence when technical correctness matters. |
| [Crossref metadata retrieval](https://www.crossref.org/documentation/retrieve-metadata/) | DOI and scholarly metadata lookup. | Metadata confirms identity; it does not validate quality. |
| [IETF RFC 2119 / RFC 8174](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/rfc8174/) | Normative keyword interpretation for technical standards language. | Applies when a specification explicitly uses those keywords. |
